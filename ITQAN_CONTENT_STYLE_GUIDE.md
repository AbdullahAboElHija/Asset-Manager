## 2. Brand Voice & Tone

### Personality Traits
- **Authoritative** — We speak as experts, not beginners. We don't ask for permission, we deliver results.
- **Precise** — No fluff, no filler words. Every sentence has purpose.
- **Bold** — We make strong claims and back them up. No hedging language ("maybe", "possibly", "we try to").
- **Technical yet Accessible** — We use technical concepts but explain them in ways business owners understand.
- **Confident, Never Arrogant** — We let our work speak. We don't put competitors down; we elevate ourselves.

### Tone Rules
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use short, punchy sentences | Write long, winding paragraphs |
| Be direct and declarative | Use passive voice or weak language |
| Use metaphors from engineering/architecture | Use generic business buzzwords |
| Speak to decision-makers (CEOs, founders, CTOs) | Sound like a beginner freelancer |
| Use numbers and specifics | Make vague promises |
| Keep it bilingual-ready (EN/AR at minimum) | Write content that only works in one language |

### Power Words (Use Frequently)
- Execution, Precision, Architecture, Engineered, Mastery
- Scale, Leverage, Autonomous, Intelligent, Structured
- Flat Vector, Hybrid Model, Zero Compromises
- Rigid, Strict Geometry, Future-Proof, Enterprise-Grade

### Words to Avoid
- "Try", "Hope", "Maybe", "Affordable", "Cheap"
- "Best in class" (generic), "Synergy" (overused), "Guru" (unprofessional)
- "We think" (replace with "We know"), "We believe" (replace with "We deliver")

## 5. Visual Content Guidelines

### Image Style
- High contrast, black and white with Electric Blue (#2563EB) accents
- Geometric shapes, grid patterns, architectural lines
- No rounded corners, no soft gradients, no organic shapes
- Clean, minimal compositions with lots of white space
- Code snippets or terminal screenshots styled in the brand palette

### Social Media Image Templates
- **Background:** Pure White (#FFFFFF) or Midnight Black (#0B1121)
- **Text:** Bold, uppercase, Inter font
- **Accent Elements:** Electric Blue lines, borders, or highlights
- **Logo Placement:** Bottom-right corner, small and clean
- **Aspect Ratios:** 1:1 (Instagram), 16:9 (LinkedIn/Twitter), 9:16 (Stories/Reels)

### Color Usage in Graphics
| Element | Color |
|---------|-------|
| Headlines | Midnight Black or Pure White (on dark bg) |
| Accent lines/borders | Electric Blue (#2563EB) |
| Background | White or Black (no greys, no gradients) |
| Body text | Black at 80% opacity or White at 80% opacity |

## 10. Bilingual Content Rules

When creating Arabic or Hebrew versions:

1. **Don't just translate — localize.** Adapt idioms and cultural references.
2. **Arabic posts should feel native**, not like translated English. Use strong Arabic verbs.
3. **Hebrew posts should be direct** — Israeli business culture values straightforwardness.
4. **Always post English + Arabic at minimum.** Hebrew is a bonus for the Israeli market.
5. **Keep the brand voice consistent** across all languages — authoritative, precise, bold.
6. **RTL formatting:** Ensure right-to-left text displays correctly on all platforms.

### Arabic Tone Notes
- Use formal modern standard Arabic (فصحى حديثة) for professionalism
- Avoid heavy dialectal expressions
- The word "إتقان" itself is a powerful Arabic brand anchor — use it in Arabic captions

### Hebrew Tone Notes
- Israeli business language is informal yet professional
- Direct communication is valued — get to the point fast
- Technical terms can stay in English (common in Israeli tech culture)
